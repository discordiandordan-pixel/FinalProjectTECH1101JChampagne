MEOW Foundation & Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR)

Supporting Calgary's Cats Through Rescue, TNR, and Community Care

About MEOW Foundation

The MEOW Foundation is a Calgary-based, no-kill cat rescue organisation dedicated to rescuing homeless cats and kittens and finding them loving homes. They also support community members who care for feral and stray cat colonies through their Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) program.

What is Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR)?

TNR is the most humane and effective strategy for managing feral cat populations. The process works in three steps:

  1. Trap — Humanely trap feral cats using live traps
  2. Neuter / Spay — Cats are taken to a vet or clinic for sterilisation and health checks
  3. Return — Cats are returned to their outdoor colony once recovered
Why TNR works
Challenge Without TNR With TNR
Population growth Continues to increase Stabilises and declines over time
Community nuisance behaviours Yowling, fighting, spraying Significantly reduced
Disease control Higher risk of disease spread Healthier, vaccinated colonies

Our Involvement

My wife and I are volunteer colony caretakers. We regularly feed the feral cats in our area and coordinate with MEOW Foundation to trap cats for spay/neuter appointments. After recovery, the cats are returned to the colony where we continue to monitor and care for them.

Trapping Safety Tips (Calgary)

  • Only set traps between April 1 and October 15, and when temperatures are 0°C to 25°C.
  • Set traps in a sheltered or shaded area.
  • Check traps more than once a day.
  • Provide food, water, and care for any trapped cat.
  • Take sick or injured cats to a vet immediately.
  • Have your trap inspected annually by a Peace Officer at the Animal Services Centre.

Source: City of Calgary - Cat Concerns
